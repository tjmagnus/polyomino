Definition 3.1.2.

A polyhedron (plural: polyhedra) is a three-dimensional closed figure in which all of the faces are polygons. Both of the solids in FigureÂ 3.1.3 are polyhedra, but the one on the left is said to be concave since we can connect some vertices, like the far left and top vertex with straight segments that pass through the exterior (outside the surface) of the solid. As we work through this chapter, we will assume that a polyhedron is convex so that it has no valleys or depressions. Any line segment connecting two points on the surface of a convex polyhedron will only contain points on the surface or interior of the polyhedron.
