Exploration 5.1.3. Recognizing a Dilation.

In Figure 5.1.6, a dilation takes \(\angle ABC\) to \(\angle DEC\text{.}\)
(a)
What is the center of the dilation?
(b)
What is the scale factor of this dilation?
(c)
Compute each of the ratios:
  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{ED}{AB}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{EC}{BC}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{CD}{CA}\)
What do you notice?
(d)
Compute the ratios:
  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{DE}{BC}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{EC}{AC}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{CD}{AB}\)
Are these the same? Why or why not?
(e)
Are any of the angles in triangle \(\Delta ABC\) congruent to an angle in \(\Delta DEC\text{?}\) Identify all pairs of congruent angles.
(f)
Recall that two triangles \(\Delta ABC\) and \(\Delta DEF\) are said to be congruent if the two triangles have three pairs of congruent corresponding angles, namely \(\angle ABC\cong \angle DEF\text{,}\) \(\angle BCA\cong \angle EFD\text{,}\) and \(\angle CAB\cong \angle FDE\text{,}\) and three pairs of congruent corresponding sides, namely \(AB=DE\text{,}\) \(BC=EF\text{,}\) and \(CA=FD\text{.}\) Are triangles \(\Delta ABC\) and \(\Delta DEC\) in Figure 5.1.6 congruent? Explain.
(g)
Two triangles \(\Delta ABC\) and \(\Delta DEF\) are said to be similar triangles if the two triangles have three pairs of congruent corresponding angles and the ratios of each of the three pairs of corresponding sides are equal; i.e., \(\angle A\cong \angle D\text{,}\) \(\angle B\cong \angle E\text{,}\) \(\angle C\cong \angle G\text{,}\) and \(\frac{DE}{AB}=\frac{EF}{BC}=\frac{FD}{CA}\text{.}\) Are the triangles in Figure 5.1.6 similar? Explain..
