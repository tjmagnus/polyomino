Exploration 5.1.1.

(a) Constructing a Dilated Quadrilateral.
The following tasks refer to Figure 5.1.2:
  1. Compute the lengths of each segment using the Pythagorean Theorem:
    1. \(\displaystyle \overline{AB}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \overline{BC}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \overline{CD}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \overline{AD}\)
  2. Use either a protractor or GeoGebra tools to measure the angles:
    1. \(\displaystyle \angle ABC\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \angle BCD\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \angle CDA\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \angle DAB\)
  3. Multiply the coordinates of each vertex by 2 to obtain coordinates for points \(A'\text{,}\) \(B'\text{,}\) \(C'\text{,}\) and \(D'\text{.}\)
    Then plot and connect the points to form quadrilateral \(A'B'C'D'\text{.}\)
(b) Relationships between the Image and the Original.
Refer to your work in Figure 5.1.2 as you answer the following:
  1. Describe how the quadrilateral \(A'B'C'D'\)you drew in Figure 5.1.2 relates to the original quadrilateral \(ABCD\text{.}\)
  2. Measure the new angles, \(\angle A'B'C'\text{,}\) \(\angle B'C'D'\text{,}\) \(\angle C'D'A'\text{,}\) and \(D'A'B'\text{.}\) How do they compare to the original four angles?
  3. Compute the lengths of the following segments
    1. \(\displaystyle \overline{A'B'}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \overline{B'C'}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \overline{C'D'}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \overline{A'D'}\)
    Compare these to the original side lengths.
  4. Let \(O\) be the point \((0,0)\text{.}\) Draw the rays \(\overrightarrow{OA'}\text{,}\) \(\overrightarrow{OB'}\text{,}\) \(\overrightarrow{OC'}\text{,}\) and \(\overrightarrow{OD'}\text{.}\) What do you notice about these rays?
