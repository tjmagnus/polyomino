Exploration 5.3.3. A Special Triangle.

In Figure 5.3.4, an isosceles triangle with two 72-degree angles is provided. Use it to answer the following:
(a)
What is the measure of the third angle?
(b)
Compute \(\frac{AC}{AB}\text{,}\) the ratio of the long side to the short side. Write the result with five significant digits.
 1 
This means to include the five digits beginning with the leftmost nonzero digit. Be sure to write the decimal point as appropriate.
.
(c)
Use the angle bisector tool to bisect angle \(\angle CAB\text{.}\) Let \(D\) be the point where this bisector intersects side \(BC\) and create line segment \(\overline{AD}\text{.}\) Use the show/hide tool to hide bisector \(\overrightarrow{AD}\text{.}\) Segment \(\overline{AD}\) should still be visible.
  1. Of the two smaller triangles created by bisecting \(\angle CAB\text{,}\) which (if any) are similar to \(\Delta ABC\text{?}\) Support your answer.
  2. Suggestion: As we continue to focus on this new smaller triangle, you may find it useful to use the create polygon tool to highlight this triangle.
    Write down the three pairs of corresponding sides.
  3. What is the constant of proportionality, \(k\) for this similarity? As you record this number, write down at least four digits after the decimal point. Adjust your calculator settings to show more digits if needed.
  4. Note that the constant of proportionality depends on whether you chose to divide the shorter length by the longer or to divide the longer length by the shorter. Perform whichever computation have not already done and record at least four digits after the decimal point.
  5. What do you notice about these two numbers?
(d)
Now use the angle bisector tool to bisect angle \(\angle ABD\) in Figure 5.3.4. Let \(E\) be the point where this angle bisector intersects segment \(\overline{AD}\text{.}\) Draw segment \(\overline{BE}\) and hide ray \(\overrightarrow{BE}\text{.}\)
  1. Name another triangle in the sketch which is similar to \(\Delta ABE\text{.}\)
  2. Name two triangles in the sketch that are similar to \(\Delta DEB\text{.}\)
(e) Reflective Questions and Extensions.
Based on your work in this exploration, discuss the following:
  1. If you use the arrow tool to move vertex \(A\) or \(B\text{,}\) the lengths of the sides of triangles will change, but the angle measures will not. Do the constants of proportionality change?
  2. Suppose you continued this process, bisecting \(\angle BDE\) to create triangle \(\Delta DEF\text{,}\) then bisecting \(\angle DEF\) to create \(\Delta EFG\text{,}\) and so forth. What type of design would you get? What properties would it have?
  3. The area tool is under the angle menu. What is the ratio of the areas of similar triangles \(\Delta ABC\) and \(\Delta BDA\text{?}\) How is this number related to the ratio of the corresponding sides?
    Use the create-polygon tool to create \(\Delta BDA\) before using the GeoGebra area tool.
