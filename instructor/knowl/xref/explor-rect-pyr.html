Exploration 3.4.2. Exploring a Rectangular Pyramid.

Throughout this activity, we will explore the rectangular pyramid that is created by folding the net in Figure 3.4.3.
(a) Constructing a pyramid from a net.
Print a copy of this net, cut it out, and fold along the dashed lines to see that this forms a rectangular pyramid. Stiffer paper such as cardstock is recommended. What do you notice about the corner at \(C\text{?}\)
Hint.
You will use this construction again near the end of this exploration. Keep it in good condition!
(b) Finding Edge Lengths and Surface Area.
Here \(ABCD\) is a rectangle with \(AD=4\) and \(CD=3\text{.}\) Angles \(\angle DCG\text{,}\) \(\angle ADH\text{,}\) \(\angle ABE\text{,}\) and \(\angle BCF\) are right angles and \(CG=4\text{.}\) Determine the following:
  1. Determine \(GD\)
  2. Determine \(BF\text{.}\)
  3. Explain why \(DH=GD\) and \(EB=BF\text{.}\)
  4. Determine \(AE\)
  5. Use your knowledge of rectangles and triangles and the definition of surface area to determine the surface area of this rectangular pyramid.
