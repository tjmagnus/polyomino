Exercise 1.
Use Figure 2.2.18 to identify the following:
  1. All labeled points that lie on segment \(\overline{EF}\text{.}\)
  2. All labeled points that lie on line \(\overleftrightarrow{EF}\text{.}\)
  3. All labeled points that lie on ray \(\overrightarrow{FE}\text{.}\)
  4. Another name for ray \(\overrightarrow{FE}\)
  5. Two other names for angle \(\angle{HEB}\text{.}\)
  6. The sum \(m(\angle{HEB})+m(\angle{AEH})\text{.}\)
  7. A point in the interior of \(\angle{CEJ}\text{.}\)
  8. Four pairs of congruent angles.
  9. All labeled points that lie on both \(\overleftrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overleftrightarrow{CE}\text{.}\)
  10. All labeled points that lie on both \(\overrightarrow{GB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{AB}\text{.}\)
Answer.
  1. Points E and F are the only labeled points that lie on the segment \(\overline{EF}\text{.}\)
  2. Points C, H, E, F, and D lie on line \(\overleftrightarrow{EF}\text{.}\)
  3. Points C, H, E, and F lie on ray \(\overrightarrow{FE}\text{.}\)
  4. Other names for \(\overrightarrow{FE}\) are \(\overrightarrow{FH}\) and \(\overrightarrow{FC}\text{.}\)
  5. Other names for \(\angle{HEB}\) are \(\angle{HEG}\text{,}\) \(\angle{CEB}\text{,}\) and \(\angle{CEG}\text{.}\)
  6. 180 degrees
  7. Point A lies in the interior of \(\angle{CEJ}\text{.}\)
  8. Several right answers; correct if the angles are vertical angles and four pairs are given. One possibility: \(\angle IEH\) and \(\angle FEJ\text{;}\) \(\angle IEB and \angle AEJ\text{;}\) \(\angle BEF\) and \(\angle HEA\text{;}\) and \(\angle IEA\) and \(\angle BEJ\text{.}\) You may choose whether to allow an angle (with multiple names) to be congruent to itself.
  9. E is the only point in common.
  10. A, B, and G all lie on both rays.
