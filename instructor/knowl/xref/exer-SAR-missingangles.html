Exercise 3.
In Figure 2.2.19,
\begin{align*} m(\angle{BED})\amp = 89^\circ\\ m(\angle{CAD})\amp = 37^\circ\\ m(\angle{DCE})\amp = 67^\circ\\ m(\angle{EFC})\amp = 52^\circ\\ m(\angle{ECF})\amp = 89^\circ \end{align*}
Determine the measures of other angles as directed.
  1. \(\displaystyle \angle{ACD}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \angle{ADC}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \angle{BCE}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \angle{BCF}\)
  5. \(\displaystyle \angle{BFC}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle \angle{CBF}\)
  7. \(\displaystyle \angle{CEF}\)
  8. \(\displaystyle \angle{ACF}\)
  9. \(\displaystyle \angle{CDE}\)
  10. What do we know about \(\angle{ABC}\) and \(\angle{BAC}\text{?}\) Explain.
Answer.
  1. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{ACD})=113^{\circ}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{ADC})=30^{\circ}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{BCE})=113^{\circ}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{BCF})=24^{\circ}\)
  5. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{BFC})=128^{\circ}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{CBF})=28^{\circ}\)
  7. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{CEF})=39^{\circ}\)
  8. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{ACF})=91^{\circ}\)
  9. \(\displaystyle m(\angle{CDE})=50^{\circ}\)
  10. The sum \(m(\angle{ABC})+m(\angle{BAC})=113^{\circ}\text{.}\) Other answers are possible with adequate support.
