Definition 2.2.3.

An angle is the union of two rays with a common endpoint. In particular, \(\angle BAC\text{,}\) shown in FigureÂ 2.2.4, consists of all points that lie on the rays \(\overrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{AC}\text{.}\) The common endpoint \(A\) of the rays is called the vertex of the angle and the two rays \(\overrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{AC}\) are called the sides of the angle.
