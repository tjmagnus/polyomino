Definition 5.1.4.

A dilation with center \(Q\) and scale factor \(k\) is a geometrical transformation that maps each point \(P\) to a point \(P'\) on line \(\overleftrightarrow{QP}\) so that \(m(\overline{QP'})=k\cdot m(\overline{QP})\text{.}\) Point \(P'\) is called the image of \(P\) under the dilation.
