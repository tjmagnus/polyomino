In addition, we observed that the translation preserved orientation, distance, angle measure, and shape. When you chose a random point on \(\overline{BC}\text{,}\) its image landed on \(\overline{B'C'}\text{,}\) suggesting that a translation should preserve linearity of points. A transformation that preserves linearity, that takes collinear points to collinear points, is called a collineation.
