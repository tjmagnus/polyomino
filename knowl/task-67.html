Task 2.4.1.c.
Perhaps you noticed that the vertex angle measure places a role in determining whether a regular polygon can be used to create a regular tiling. This task will lead you to determine the measure of the vertex angle of a regular pentagon. We will use this technique to find the measure of the vertex angles for other regular n-gons.
  • \(\displaystyle \angle 1 = \angle EAD\)
  • \(\displaystyle \angle 2 = \angle DAC\)
  • \(\displaystyle \angle 3 = \angle CAB\)
  • \(\displaystyle \angle 4 = \angle EDA\)
  • \(\displaystyle \angle 5 = \angle ADC\)
  • \(\displaystyle \angle 6 = \angle DCA\)
  • \(\displaystyle \angle 7 = \angle ACB\)
  • \(\angle E = \angle DEA\text{,}\) etc.
  1. Explain why \(m\angle EAB=m\angle 1+m\angle 2+m\angle 3\)
  2. What is \(m\angle 1 + m\angle 4 + m\angle E\text{?}\)
  3. Use the fact that the pentagon is divided into triangles to determine \(m\angle EAB+m\angle B+m\angle BCD+m\angle CDE +m\angle E\text{.}\)
  4. Having just computed the sum of the five congruent angles of a regular pentagon, determine \(m\angle A\text{.}\)
  5. Some of what you found, but not all, will be true for all convex pentagons. What results about angle measure will also hold for non-regular convex pentagons? What will be different?
