Exploration 2.1.1. Discovering an Area Formula for a Parallelogram.

Refer to the definition of parallogram Definition 1.3.12 as needed to complete the following.
(a)
Sketch the following:
  1. A parallelogram with two different angle measures and two different lengths.
  2. A parallelogram with a least one acute angle and four congruent sides.
  3. A parallelogram with four congruent angles. What do we usually call this parallelogram?
  4. Two parallelograms which share the same side lengths but have different heights.
(b)
Beginning with the parallelogram pictured below or the one you sketched in Item 1, explain and demonstrate how you would construct a rectangle with the same area. You are allowed to cut the parallelogram and rearrange or duplicate pieces as part of your argument.
(c)
Building on the previous problem, derive an area formula for a parallelogram. Explain how it comes from the area formula for a rectangle and how you know it must work for all parallelograms.
