Checkpoint 2.4.10. Key Ideas related to Regular Polygons.

  1. What formula can be used to compute the angle measure for a regular \(n\)-gon?
  2. What is the sum of the measures of the vertex angles for any (not-necessarily regular) convex \(n\)-gon? For example, the measures of the angles of any triangle add up to 180 degrees. What is the total for a convex quadrilateral, a convex pentagon, etc.?
  3. Describe the three regular tilings.
  4. If a group of polygons meet at a single vertex, what must be the sum of the angle measures at that vertex? Note that this wording generalizes this fact to nonregular polygons as well.
  5. Just because a collection of regular polygons can surround a vertex does not mean that they can create a semiregular tiling. Give an example of a vertex arrangement that does not extend to a regular tiling. Support your claim.
