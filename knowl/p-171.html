We now have four area formulas in our tool box. One could continue to add more: kites, pentagons, hexagons, etc., but the list would become unwieldly and unhelpful. Instead, we will simply break other polygonal shapes into triangles, parallelograms, and other shapes so that we can compute the area of these and add (or subtract) the areas as appropriate. Measuring tools could be used to estimate the dimensions needed to compute the area of physical spaces such as floorspace or a garden.
