Exercise 8. Area Model for Two-digit Numbers.
Multiplication of two-digit numbers can be represented using the area model: for example, to multiply 47 by 25 we can represent 47 as 4 tens and 7 ones and 25 as 2 tens and 5 ones. We then can sketch the product as shown.
  1. Determine the area of each of the four subrectangles pictured. Here, the squares on the grid are not \(1\times 1\) but defined by the notations on the sides.
  2. Add the four areas of the subrectangles together to find the total area.
