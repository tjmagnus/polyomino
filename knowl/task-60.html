Task 2.4.1.e.
Returning to the question of when a regular polygon can be used to create a regular tiling,
  1. How many equilateral triangles can meet at a single point?
  2. How many squares can meet at a single point?
  3. How many regular hexagons can meet at a single point?
  4. Explain in words and pictures why these three regular polygons can each produce a regular tiling.
  5. When we tried to surround a point with regular pentagons a gap was formed. What is the degree measure of that gap?
  6. If \(n\geq 7\text{,}\) do you know whether a regular \(n\)-gon might produce a regular tiling? Include a discussion of vertex angle measure in your explanation.
