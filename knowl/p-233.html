On the other hand, each student brings their own individual strengths to the class. Some are great at remembering and applying formulas, some are great at drawing sketches, some recognize patterns, some build on others’ ideas, some explain results and connections. In fact, you may find yourself contributing in different ways on different activities. The important thing is to believe in yourself and be open to trying new things. Unfortunately, most people believe the lie that they can’t do math and spend too much energy comparing themselves to others. Math is not about being perfect or fast. Instead it is about exploring, creating, experimenting, conjecturing, applying, and justifying.
