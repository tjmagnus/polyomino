Exercise 4.
In Figure 1.2.27, three smaller Tangram pieces fill a region congruent to the larger blue isosceles triangle. The square is half as tall as the blue triangle. Suppose that the area of the blue triangle is 18 square inches. Refer to this figure to answer the following questions:
(a)
What is the area of one small orange triangle? How do you know?
Hint.
To find the area of one small orange triangle, consider how many of these small triangles would fit into the larger blue isosceles triangle.
(b)
What is the area of the square? How do you know?
Hint.
Refer to the given information regarding the area of the blue triangle and the height of the green square. Consider how this information can be used to determine the area of the green square.
