Exercise 4.
In Figure 1.2.29, three smaller Tangram pieces fill a region congruent to the larger blue isosceles triangle. The square is half as tall as the blue triangle. Suppose that the area of the blue triangle is 18 square inches. Refer to this figure to answer the following questions:
(a)
What is the area of one small orange triangle? How do you know?
Answer.
Each small orange triangle has an area of 4.5 square inches.
Solution.
The blue triangle has an area of 18 square inches. The green square and two orange triangles together also total 18 square inches. Since the green square is 9 square inches, the remaining 9 is split equally between the two orange triangles. Therefore, each orange triangle has an area of 4.5 square inches.
(b)
What is the area of the square? How do you know?
Answer.
The area of the green square is 9 square inches.
Solution.
The green square is half the height of the blue triangle but has the same base. This means its area is half of the blue triangle’s area. Therefore, the square has an area of 9 square inches.
