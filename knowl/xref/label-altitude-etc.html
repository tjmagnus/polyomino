Triangle \(\Delta ABC\text{,}\) trapezoid \(DEGF\text{,}\) and parallelogram \(HKJI\) are provided in FigureÂ 1.2.26.
(a)
Label a side of triangle \(\Delta ABC\) as the base. Then draw and label the altitude of the triangle corresponding to that base.
Hint.
Any of the three sides of a triangle can be chosen as the base. Once chosen, draw a perpendicular line segment from that base to the opposite vertex.
(b)
Which two sides of trapezoid \(DEGF\) could be considered to be bases? Label one as the base. Also label the summit and legs of the trapezoid. Then draw and label an altitude for \(DEGF\text{.}\)
Hint.
A trapezoid has one pair of parallel sides, which are the bases. The altitude is the perpendicular distance between these bases.
(c)
Label side \(\overline{HI}\) as the base of parallelogram \(HIJK\text{.}\) Sketch and label an altitude for \(HIJK\) corresponding to base \(\overline{HI}\text{.}\)
Hint.
In a parallelogram, opposite sides are parallel and equal. Draw a line segment that is perpendicular to both the base (side \(\overline{HI}\)) and its opposite side.
(d)
Could another side of \(HIJK\) been chosen as the base? Which one(s)? Would the altitude be the same?
Hint.
In a parallelogram, there are two pairs of opposite sides. How does the choice of a base pair affect the altitude (height) of a parallelogram?
