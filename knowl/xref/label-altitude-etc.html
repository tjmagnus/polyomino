Exercise 3.
Triangle \(\Delta ABC\text{,}\) trapezoid \(DEGF\text{,}\) and parallelogram \(HKJI\) are provided in Figure 1.2.24.
  1. Label a side of triangle \(\Delta ABC\) as the base. Then draw and label the altitude of the triangle corresponding to that base.
  2. Which two sides of trapezoid \(DEGF\) could be considered to be bases? Label one as the base. Also label the summit and legs of the trapezoid. Then draw and label an altitude for \(DEGF\text{.}\)
  3. Label side \(\overline{HI}\) as the base of parallelogram \(HIJK\text{.}\) Sketch and label an altitude for \(HIJK\) corresponding to base \(\overline{HI}\text{.}\)
  4. Could another side of \(HIJK\) been chosen as the base? Which one(s)? Would the altitude be the same?
Hint.
Check that each altitude is perpendicular to the corresponding base. Multiple answers are possible for most of these questions.
