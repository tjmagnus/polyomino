Exercise 3. Visualizing the Volume Formula for a Rectangular Pyramid.
Print the three nets in Figure 3.4.12 on stiff paper. The scale factor of any enlargements should be consistent. Cut out the nets and fold each to create a rectangular pyramid, taping the edges as needed.
  1. Use the formula \(V=\frac{Ad}{3}\) to determine the volume of each pyramid. Note that two pyramids should be identical.
  2. Put the three pyramids together so that they form a \(3\times 4\times 4\)-rectangular prism. How does this demonstrate that the formula \(V=\frac{Ad}{3}\) holds for this particular pyramid?
