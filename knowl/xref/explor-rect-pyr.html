Exploration 3.4.2. Exploring a Rectangular Pyramid.

In this activity, you will explore the rectangular pyramid that is created by folding only one of the nets in Figure 3.4.4, Figure 3.4.5, or Figure 3.4.6.
(a) Finding Edge Lengths and Surface Area.
Regardless of which Pyramid Net you are working wih, \(ABCD\) is a rectangle: either \(3\times 4\) (Pyramid Net 1 3.4.4), \(3\times 5\) (Pyramid Net 2 3.4.5), or \(4\times 5\) (Pyramid Net 3 3.4.6). Angles \(\angle CAE\text{,}\) \(\angle BAF\text{,}\) \(\angle DCE'\text{,}\) and \(\angle DBF'\) are right angles and the length of \(AE\) is provided. Determine the following:
(i)
Identify the Pyramid Net, Net 1 Figure 3.4.4, Net 2 Figure 3.4.5, or Net 3 Figure 3.4.6, that will be the focus of your work.
(ii)
Determine \(EC\text{.}\)
(iii)
Determine \(AF\text{.}\)
(iv)
Determine \(BF\text{.}\)
(v)
Explain without performing any calculations why \(CE'=CE\) and \(BF'=BF\text{.}\)
(vi)
Calculate \(DE'\) and \(DF'\text{.}\)
(vii)
Use your knowledge of area of rectangles and triangles and the definition of surface area to determine the surface area of this rectangular pyramid.
