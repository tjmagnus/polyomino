This problem illustrates the Area Model for Multiplication, an effective way of helping elementary school students visualize the operation of multiplication.
  1. On graph paper sketch a \(3 \times 5\) rectangle. Show the fifteen squares that make up the area.
  2. Note that 12 can be factored as \(1 \times 12\text{,}\) \(2 \times 6\text{,}\) or \(3 \times 4\text{.}\) Sketch area models for each of these products.
