The above exploration starts off relatively easy. Once students understand how to create and measure shapes on the geoboard, most can quickly find triangles with areas of \(\frac{1}{2}, 1, \frac{3}{2}, 2, 3\text{,}\) and \(\frac{9}{2},\text{.}\) In fact, they find multiple triangles with these areas. Yet the remaining areas may seem impossible. At some point they begin to realize why they keep finding triangles with these five areas and begin to think differently about how they might create triangles on the geoboard.
