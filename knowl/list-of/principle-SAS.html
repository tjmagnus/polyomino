Principle 4.3.9. SAS Triangle Congruence.

If two triangles have two pairs of congruent corresponding sides and the corresponding angles between these sides is also congruent, then the triangles are congruent. In particular, if \(\Delta ABC\) and \(\Delta DEF\) have \(AB=DE\) and \(BC=EF\) and \(\angle ABC\cong\angle DEF\text{,}\) then \(\Delta ABC\cong\Delta DEF\text{.}\)
