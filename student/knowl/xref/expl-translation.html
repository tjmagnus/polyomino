Exploration 4.2.1. Exploring a Translation.

Figure Figure 4.2.2 shows an example of the action of a translation on triangle \(\Delta ABC\text{.}\) Use this figure to answer the following:
(a)
Does the transformation mapping \(\Delta ABC\) to \(\Delta A'B'C'\) preserve distance? In other words, is the length of each side of \(\Delta A'B'C'\) equal to its corresponding side in \(\Delta ABC\text{?}\)
(b)
Are the corresponding angles of \(\Delta ABC\) to \(\Delta A'B'C'\) congruent?
(c)
Is the orientation of the triangle \(\Delta ABC\) preserved under the translation? How do you know?
(d)
Sketch the segments \(\overline{AA'}\text{,}\) \(\overline{BB'}\text{,}\) and \(\overline{CC'}\text{.}\)
  1. What do you notice about the lengths of these segments?
  2. What appears to be true about the lines \(\overleftrightarrow{AA'}\text{,}\) \(\overleftrightarrow{BB'}\text{,}\) and \(\overleftrightarrow{CC'}\text{?}\)
Hint.
Since lines have infinite length, you should be looking for a property other than length.
